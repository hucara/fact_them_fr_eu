A private jet emits between 5 and 40 times more CO2 than a commercial flight.
85% confidence
EnvironmentInternational
Omissions
  • The specific multiplier varies significantly based on: (1) aircraft type - light jets have higher per-passenger emissions than heavy jets; (2) occupancy - private jets often fly with only 1-4 passengers vs 100-300 on commercial flights; (3) flight distance - short flights have disproportionately higher emissions per km due to takeoff/climb phases. Some studies cite figures up to 500 times more for frequent private jet users on an annual basis. The ICCT 2023 report provides detailed data but uses 2023 data which was available before the claim date.
Sources
  • PrimaryInternational Council on Clean Transportation (ICCT) - Air and greenhouse gas pollution from private jets, 2023Private jets emit significantly more GHGs per passenger than commercial aviation; a typical private jet emits about 810 tonnes of GHGs annually. Emission intensity varies by aircraft class and occupancy.
  • SecondaryEconomic Times - Private jet carbon emission studyPrivate jets emit 14 times more carbon per passenger than commercial flights; frequent private jet users emit 500 times more CO2 annually compared to average commercial flight passengers.
  • SecondaryDataboks/Katadata - Private jet carbon emissions comparisonEmission intensity of private jets is approximately ten times higher at 1,300 grams of CO2 per passenger per kilometer flown compared to commercial airliners.
