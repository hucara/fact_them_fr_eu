Almost a quarter of children are still subject to poverty
95% confidence
Social PolicyEuropean Union
Omissions
  • The precise figure is 24.2%, not 25%, though this falls within normal rounding tolerance for 'almost a quarter'.
  • The data refers to the 'at risk of poverty or social exclusion' (AROPE) indicator, which is broader than income poverty alone - it includes people at risk of poverty, severely materially and socially deprived, or living in households with very low work intensity.
  • The 2024 data was published by Eurostat in May 2025, which would have been available at the time of the January 2026 claim.
  • The figure decreased from 24.8% in 2023 to 24.2% in 2024, showing a slight improvement trend.
Sources
