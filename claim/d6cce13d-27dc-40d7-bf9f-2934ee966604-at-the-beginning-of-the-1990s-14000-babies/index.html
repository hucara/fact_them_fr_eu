Overestimated
At the beginning of the 1990s, 14,000 babies were infected with HIV through nosocomial transmission in hospitals.
85% confidence
HealthRomania
Omissions
  • The commonly cited figure is approximately 11,000 children infected, not 14,000
  • The 14,000 figure may be confused with the number of children in Romanian orphanages (a figure mentioned in contemporary reports)
  • The infections occurred primarily between 1986 and 1990, affecting children in institutional care
  • By mid-2002, official Romanian registry data showed 9,936 children with HIV/AIDS, of which 11,000 is considered the total cohort of pediatric nosocomial infections from that period
  • The epidemic was caused by poor medical practices including reuse of needles, microtransfusions, and lack of sterilization in pediatric and obstetric hospitals
