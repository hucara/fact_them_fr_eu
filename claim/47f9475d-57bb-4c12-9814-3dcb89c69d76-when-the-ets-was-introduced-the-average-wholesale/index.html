Omissions
  • The claim uses 'today' (March 2026) but prices in early 2026 had decreased significantly from 2022 peaks; 85 EUR/MWh appears to reference 2022-2023 crisis levels rather than March 2026 prices
  • The 30-40 EUR/MWh figure for 2005 is slightly underestimated - actual prices were closer to 30-50 EUR/MWh depending on the market
  • The claim does not specify which European market is being referenced (German, French, EU average) - prices vary significantly by country
  • The speaker could not have known March 2026 prices at the time of the speech (March 25, 2026) as this data would not yet be available
