The European Union is experiencing the longest period without an accession of new member states
95% confidence
Internal AffairsEuropean Union
Omissions
  • The claim is accurate as of the date it was made (9 March 2026). The previous longest period without enlargement was between 1 January 1995 and 1 May 2004 (8 years and 4 months). The current gap since Croatia's accession on 1 July 2013 exceeds this.
  • The European Commission's official enlargement page confirms Croatia as the most recent member state to join the EU.
  • Source publication dates are after the claim date (2026-03-09) but contain historical data about past enlargements that would have been known to the MEP.
