Omissions
  • The 20% figure refers to global data from the ILO Global Wage Report 2018/19, which is now several years old
  • The EU gender pay gap is significantly lower at approximately 11-12% according to 2022-2024 Eurostat data
  • The speaker cited 'International' scope but was speaking in the European Parliament where EU-specific data (11-12%) would be more relevant than the global figure
  • The 20% figure is an unadjusted pay gap that does not account for differences in occupation, hours worked, or experience
