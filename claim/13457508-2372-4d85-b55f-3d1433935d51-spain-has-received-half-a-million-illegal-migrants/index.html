Omissions
  • The claim fails to specify whether it refers to total undocumented population (stock) or recent arrivals (flow)
  • The 500,000 figure appears to reference a proposed regularization policy, not recent migration data
  • The most recent data available before February 2026 shows approximately 837,938 undocumented migrants in Spain (stock) but only around 34,000 irregular arrivals in the first 11 months of 2025 (flow)
  • The BBC article was published in 2025, which the MEP could have known, but the specific data period varies by interpretation
