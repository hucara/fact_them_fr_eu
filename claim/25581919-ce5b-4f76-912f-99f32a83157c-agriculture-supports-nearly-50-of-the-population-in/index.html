The claim that agriculture supports 'nearly 50%' of the population in Réunion is factually incorrect. Official INSEE statistics show that the agriculture, forestry and fishing sector represents only approximately 3-4% of employment in Réunion.

The services/tertiary sector actually accounts for 83% of employment in Réunion, not agriculture.
Omissions
  • The claim significantly overstates the importance of agriculture in Réunion's economy. The actual employment in agriculture represents a small fraction (3-4%) of the working population, not 50%. The MEP may be conflating agriculture with other economic sectors or referring to an outdated historical period when sugar cane production was more dominant.
  • The tertiary (services) sector accounts for 83% of employment in Réunion according to 2023 INSEE data.
  • INSEE data from 2022 shows a total population of approximately 868,800 inhabitants in Réunion.
