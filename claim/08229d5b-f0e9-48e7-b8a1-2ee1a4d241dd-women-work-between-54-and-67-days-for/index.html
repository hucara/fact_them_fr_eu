Omissions
  • The current EU average gender pay gap is 12-13% (2024-2025), corresponding to approximately 44-47 unpaid days per year, not 54-67 days.
  • The claim does not specify whether it refers to EU average or specific countries with higher gaps.
  • The European Commission's Equal Pay Day 2025 was November 17, marking 44 days of symbolically unpaid work.
  • Historical EU gender pay gap data shows improvement: from ~17-18% in 2010 to 12-13% in 2024-2025.
  • The UK TUC reported 52 days for 2024 based on 14.3% gap, but the UK is no longer in the EU.
  • Some individual EU countries have higher gaps (e.g., Estonia ~18%), which could correspond to ~65-66 days, but this is not the EU average.
