In France, 12 weeks are needed between diagnosis and treatment due to lack of specialists, whereas recommendations advise acting within six weeks
70% confidence
HealthFrance
Omissions
  • The 12-week figure appears to be specific to certain cancer types (particularly thoracic cancers) and measures from 'suspicion' to treatment rather than strictly 'diagnosis' to treatment
  • Data from October 2024 shows shorter average waiting times for common cancers: 23.4 days for colorectal cancer and 40 days for breast cancer
  • The claim does not specify that this data refers specifically to cancer care rather than general medical conditions
  • The 6-week recommendation mentioned could not be verified from official HAS (Haute Autorité de Santé) sources
