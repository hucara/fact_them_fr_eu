Almost three million people in Europe receive a cancer diagnosis every year
85% confidence
HealthEurope
Omissions
  • The figure of ~3 million refers specifically to the EU-27 (European Union), not the entire European continent. The Europe region including non-EU countries has approximately 4.47 million new cancer cases per year according to IARC/WHO data.
  • The most recent ECIS (European Cancer Information System) data shows 2.74 million cases for 2022 and 2.7 million cases for 2024 in EU Member States, slightly below 3 million.
  • The source with the closest figure (2.99 million) is the IARC Global Cancer Observatory, not the EU's own European Cancer Information System (ECIS).
  • The JRC news article providing 2024 data was published on 2025-12-17, which is after the session date (2026-02-09 is actually after this publication - correction: 2026-02-09 is in February 2026, so the December 2025 publication is BEFORE the session date).
