Cancer remains the second cause of death in the European Union
95% confidence
HealthEuropean Union
Omissions
  • The most recent available data at the time of the claim (February 2026) is from 2022, published by Eurostat in 2024.
  • Diseases of the circulatory system (including heart attacks and strokes) are consistently the leading cause of death in the EU, accounting for about 32.7% of deaths in 2022.
  • Cancer accounts for approximately 1.2 million deaths annually in the EU.
  • The ranking reverses for people under 65 years: cancer becomes the leading cause of death in this age group.
Sources
  • PrimaryEurostat - Causes of death statisticsIn 2022, diseases of the circulatory system accounted for 32.7% of all deaths in the EU (leading cause), while cancer was the second most common cause at 22.3%.
  • PrimaryEurostat - Cancer statistics news releaseIn 2021, cancer was the leading cause of death for both men and women aged less than 65 years in the EU.
  • SecondaryVoronoi - Europe's Top Causes of Death, RankedCirculatory diseases are the most common causes of death in the EU, claiming 1.7 million lives or 33% of all deaths, with cancer as the second leading cause.
