Omissions
  • The most recent FAO data available before the claim date (February 12, 2026) showed 673 million people facing hunger in 2024, which is slightly below 700 million. The 2023 figure of 733 million was higher than 700 million. The claim does not specify which year or data source it refers to.
  • FAO uses the term 'undernourishment' or 'facing hunger' rather than 'starving', which has a more specific clinical meaning.
  • The data shows global hunger actually declined from 733 million in 2023 to 673 million in 2024, which provides important context about the trend.
